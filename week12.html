Testing our UI

We met Mahfuzul K. from Strathfield Council today to see what he thinks of our current app's UX and UI. Mahfuzul said that the app is good, and that the infringement structure and menu is better than before. He stated that the personal record section is good and that he expected to click on the infringement notice cancellation button if there was a mistake. However, towards the last page of the infringement notice section (the print record), he tried to zoom in, which indicates that the text is small at a first glance. Despite this, he said he was able to read it.


Reflection
I think we need to improve the overall aesthetics of our interface to make it look more realistic and professional. Being a designer has taught me that colour harmony plays an important role in user interface design. With that said, the colour scheme of our current app is not that pleasant and the icons do not seem to go hand-in-hand with each other.

To improve this, we need to re-evaluate our colour scheme and possibly change the splash screen and the map colour scheme. We need to somehow improve this before we submit our video tomorrow night.

Reflection
Written the day after
The video we submitted yesterday night was not our very best. Unfortunately that night, we had a change of mind about how we wanted our final design to look like. We had to reshoot the part where we filmed our app interface. Since it was late at night, it was not consistent with the lighting throughout the video. That's why the video didn't turn out as great as we thought it would be.

Despite this, I learned that for the next video assignment (due next week) we need to shoot as much as we can during the daytime because lighting plays such a big role. In addition to this, I believe that I need to be more organised in scheduling our group meetups.
