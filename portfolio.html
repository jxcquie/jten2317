drone on patrol_

An autonomous drone mobile app aiming to improve and revolutionise the parking enforcement industry.
a new way
Creating a platform for Sydney councils to improve the efficiency of parking enforcement through an autonomous technology.

the challenge
Our goal for the project was to create a prototype that addresses the current problems in parking enforcement based from our background research.

Our main goals were to:
− Create an effective mobile app for parking rangers
− Provide an incentive for rangers to be on duty
− Automate areas that need efficiency

My role:
I was appointed as the lead design in our group of four.
I was responsible for conducting background research, creating designs, prototyping and testing with users.



the research
I contacted several councils in Sydney to see whether they could assist with our group's research. We managed to get a hold of Strathfield, Burwood and City of Sydney Council. We interviewed 5 rangers.

Our primary goal for the research was to get a better understanding of what happens on and off duty, and problematic areas that could be improved.

Problematic areas we encountered:
− Work/on-duty productivity
− Ethical issues
− Internal and external concerns








the concepts
Our group created three different concepts.




1. Mobile Drone Patrol Parking App
The drone technology is programmed to fly and check through certain roads and areas for a certain amount of time. When the drone technology detects that a car has breached the parking limit, the drone’s camera gps will send an alert to the parking officer’s mobile app which then allows the officer to identify where the car’s location is in order to conduct a proper documentation to file in court.


2. Parking Drone Management App
The parking drone management concept utilises the drone’s GPS and camera sensors to detect when a car has overstayed its park. The drone is programmed to fly to certain routes every hour to detect cars whilst the manager controls and programs the drone from the office in real-time. The drone can then take the camera and GPS information to fill in the infringement notice.


3. Parking Management App for Drivers
The third concept is a mobile app for drivers to find parking, top up on metered parking and be alerted of their parking status from the drones’ surveys. Unlike the previous two concepts, it focuses on improving accuracy and management of parking rules on the driver’s side.
storyboards
We created three storyboards for our three concepts.
annotated paper prototype
Before we undertook our lo-fi prototype, we made an annotated paper prototype for each of our concepts in order to visualise how each experience will look like.
low fidelity
prototype
After we annotated the paper prototypes, we created three lo-fi prototypes of our concepts.


We evaluated our three lofi prototypes using the tools think aloud, heuristic evaluation and usability testing. From this, we found that our users understood the essential steps needed to accomplish a task. However, improvements needed to be made of the interface structure and layout.
high fidelity
By this stage of the design process, iterations were completed and evaluation tests were conducted for the three designs. However, in this stage of the design process we had to select one concept to pursue for our final design and prototype.

Our group were highly influenced by the ranger's thoughts and understanding of our prototypes hence after discussing our prototypes and the areas that needed improvement, we decided to create another round of iteration in a high fidelity form to see if the rangers agree on a final design.

We focused on iterating Concept 1 and Concept 2.
final design
In the end, we chose to iterate on concept one - the mobile drone patrol app for parking officers for our final concept.
The mobile drone patrol app was the most successful of our earlier tests as the interface and process were easily understood by the parking officers.

Our final mobile drone patrol app incorporates previous feedback and aspects of concept two to provide a more flexible and efficient use of drones to aid in parking enforcement.
