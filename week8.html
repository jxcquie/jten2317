Interview at Burwood Council

We had an interview with a Senior Manager Compliance named Martin Jenner from Burwood Council on Wednesday the 26th of September 2018. The interview was about an hour long and had taken place in Burwood Council’s private room. Martin is very knowledgeable in his field, and it was clear throughout the interview that he was passionate about our idea and the need to push for innovation and progress in the parking enforcement area. Our group had learned a lot just by listening to Martin speak throughout the interview. Martin was kind enough to let us record the interview, however, we did not take pictures as we wanted to keep his privacy as well as we wanted to be respectful during the entire interview.

Essentially, Martin talked about how the current parking enforcement system is slow and that he believes the only way to efficiently and effectively improve the system is to entirely get rid of human interaction. Martin expressed how people are not 100% all the time, therefore, it is difficult for the current system to be entirely efficient. For example, Martin described how eight of his officers have to take annual/sick leave which meant that he is left with only 4 productive months of the year. Similarly, he described that not every day will be a productive day for the officers as internal and external matters such as one’s energy levels or hot/cold weather could overall affect their day on the job. In addition, Martin mentioned about how there are a lot of ethical issues surrounding parking enforcement officers, as parking officers could be biased towards certain people just because they know they might get abused, or that they know someone and will be hesitant to give them a fine even if they have breached the law. Overall, Martin expressed how there are a lot of issues with human interaction in the parking enforcement sector and that he believes autonomous devices/vehicles could incredibly enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of the workload.
