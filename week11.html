Designing UI and feedback

Today we discussed with Hollie about how our prototype could improve from the last assessment. Hollie stated that we should think about incorporating other useful features into our app. For example, including a feature where officers can see problem areas in their district, and notifying officers nearby of a breach in parking

In addition, Hollie mentioned some small interface details that we need to improve on. For example, our menu page was not clear, there was no clear contrast on the screen, and the images need to be more consistent and clear.

Reflection
When we talked to Hollie about our prototype from the last assignment/presentation, Hollie brought to our attention that we did make a hi-fi prototype instead of a low-fi prototype. We were highly aware of this and that we regret not doing things properly and panicking due to the time we had. It was unfortunate that our group were too focused on the evaluation methods instead of the prototype in itself. In the future, we’ll definitely learn to not repeat the same mistakes again. Speaking of not repeating the same mistakes again, we understand that we made the mistake of testing in an office room instead of out in the public. We will definitely test out in the streets for our next assignment to ensure that we replicate our ideal scenario in the real world.
Designing UI

We started creating our screens for our iteration. After listening to Hollie’s advice on Thursday, we made sure that we had at least 3 clear and distinct features that officers can use. The three features we chose to have are:

Admin View for checking drones - concerns battery life of drones, consistency






Notifying nearby officers of a breach in parking




Issuing a fine










Iteration/changes we made:
  • The contrast between admin view and ranger view
  • Distinct green coloured button to indicate an on-click event
  • Images that appear more realistic on the app

Reflection
I believe that our current iteration looks better in terms of the interface and usability. However, I do think that the icons could be more improved to look realistic. In addition, I am not a big fan of the map aesthetic of our current app, I do hope that we improve on the map visuals in our next iteration.
