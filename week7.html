Background Research

Due to the limited amount of time our group has, our group is hoping to interview a few officers/councils so that our group has some form of information to work from. I had contacted several councils yesterday to see whether they could assist our group with the Parking Officer research that we are conducting. So far out of the five emails I had sent, two have responded. The questions I intended to ask are:

  • What is the typical day/process of a parking officer?

  • What are some of the duties of parking officers?

  • What are some of the biggest issues that parking officers experience on patrol duties?

  • What are the things that parking officers are doing to make their on-patrol duties easier?

  • What tools do parking officers use in their work?


Aside from this, our group has done individual second-hand research to understand the roles and duties of parking officers, and the problems they encounter on the job.
Parking officers ensure that drivers follow local parking laws and issue fines for violations. They typically are assigned to a particular geographic area (a local council) to patrol and monitor violations such as a no-parking zone, 2P, expired meters, and more. They are responsible for preparing reports and filing paperwork, and often would have to go to the court to present. Aside from this, parking officers do encounter a difficult challenge: they are often physically and verbally abused by people.


Reflection
From our understanding of parking officers, based from our research, we created two ideas that we hope to turn into design concepts using storyboards.
Phone call interview

I had contacted a Senior Regulatory Officer-Ranger named David M. from Strathfield Council earlier on the week to see if he could help our group with our research. We arranged a phone call interview for Friday the 21st of September, 2018 (which was yesterday) and the phone call interview was approximately 24 minutes long. Our group got to ask all of the questions that we had prepared to ask. Gaby (Gabrielle) was the interviewer of the phone call interview with David, while I was listening next to her, trying to write down notes on my phone. Unfortunately, in this interview we were unable to record our phone call with David because of consent and privacy issues. My quick notes during the phone call interview: (I was unable to record word-to-word)
  • What are the duties of a parking officer?
    Patrol the streets regarding parking, keep everything within road rules, time parking, patrolling
  • What are the strategies/techniques you use for finding parking violators?
    Chalk the tyres for time, record in our notebooks. We use to write in our notebooks but now we use an electronic PDA using the Samsung Note 5 phone version.
  • What are some of the difficulties you encounter on the job?
    Weather. If it’s extreme cold or extreme heat. The surface on the ground that we’re walking on/street concrete. Walking up and down the hill. Customers, all types of customers. We get verbal/physical abuse
  • What is your typical day/process as a parking officer?
    Walk in, drive in, hop in the car to go to another suburb, there’s a lot of walking. Sometimes there might be a complaint being sent.
  • How do you know where to go?
    You have a map, or you know the areas where to go. Health/weather or natural stuff can happen
  • Can you tell me more about the PDA?
    It is a program that give prompts. So on the main menu it prompts street/council parking, general council, dog management.
    Street - issue pin, review, rego
    What kind of plate: nsw, vic, qld
    What kind of vehicle it is
    Offence code: e.g. disobey $230 (can change)
    Offence time/date: location, street name/suburb
    Vehicle make: body/sudan/hatch/colour
    Operational details like client code: a council has a code that relates to the council (for e.g. Strathfield Council has their own for when they lodge to the government)
    Tickets are printed out of all the details as long as you hit the next page, it prompts you to the next
    Different signage for different days (it is quite a good system)
  • What are your thoughts on autonomous enforcement drones?
    Some councils already use camera cars. I don’t know about the drone idea. I think it’ll be good in the future but for now I think that the community won’t accept it. We already have the parking ticket PDA system. Unless the camera on the drone is so strong and can scan four streets at once or whether it can see behind bushes, I just don’t see the point of having it.


After the first phone call interview that we did, we wanted to go to Town Hall council to see if we could talk to more officers. When we reached Town Hall council, we saw three parking officers who were on their break. We kindly approached them to see if we could ask them quick questions for our research. They agreed and we were ready to ask our questions. One officer had left so in the end we interviewed two officers: a male officer named Edwin and a female officer named Vicki. Unfortunately again, we couldn’t record or take pictures due to consent and privacy. We had agreed not to record or take pictures of our short interview. I was the interviewer whilst Gaby and Sandra (Jihyun) were recording the answers.


Reflection
Overall, I think we had a successful day. We managed to conduct a phone call interview, as well as interview two officers that we had just met in Town Hall. We were not expecting any officer to help us after our phone call interview, but I’m glad that we approached the two parking officers while they were on their break (they were just sitting down relaxing, so we didn’t really ruin their break).
In both of the interviews that we did, we asked the officers of what they thought about autonomous enforcement drones. It seems that they were not on board with the idea of having drones flying around taking their jobs but I think it is because we failed in explaining how it could incredibly help them with their work, and not just to solely take their jobs away from them. Next time, we’ll have a better explanation of what we are trying to do, with better intentions.
